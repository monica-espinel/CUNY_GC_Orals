Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil 1937–1970, 1978–1980 Birthplace and primary home. Trained with Ivan Serpa, joined Grupo Frente (1954–56) and co-founded the Neo-Concrete movement (1959). His immersion in the Mangueira favela and samba school profoundly shaped his participatory works. Metaesquemas (1957–58), Bólides (1963–69), Parangolés (1964–), Tropicália (1967)
London, UK 1968–1969 Major exhibition at Whitechapel Gallery introduced his work to international audiences. Exposure to British pop and conceptual art scenes. Eden installation at Whitechapel Gallery (1969)
New York, USA 1970–1978 Lived in exile during Brazilian military dictatorship. Engaged with underground counterculture. Created environmental and film works, including Quasi-Cinemas. Ninhos (Nests), Cosmococa series, Quasi-Cinema
