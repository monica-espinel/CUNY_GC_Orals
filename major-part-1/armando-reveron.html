Armando Reverón

Armando Reverón (1889–1954) was one of the most influential Venezuelan artists of the 20th century, celebrated for his unique vision of light and atmosphere. Living reclusively at El Castillete in Macuto, he developed an extraordinary body of work characterized by three distinct periods: Blue, White, and Sepia. His innovative approach to capturing tropical light made him a precursor to modern art movements.
