Camilo Egas

Camilo Egas (1889–1962) was one of Ecuador's most significant 20th-century artists, renowned for his pioneering role in the Indigenismo art movement and his contributions to modern art in both South America and the United States. He served as Director of the Art Department at The New School in New York for nearly 30 years.

Quito, Ecuador 1889 (birth); early career 1926 Birthplace in San Blas neighborhood. Studied at Escuela de Bellas Artes. Founded Indigenist Movement upon return from Europe. Launched Ecuador's first art periodical, Helice. El san juanito (1917), Grupo de Indios: danza ceremonial (1922)
Rome, Madrid, Paris 1911–1925 Trained at Royal Academy (Rome, 1911), Real Academia de San Fernando (Madrid, 1919), Académie Colarossi (Paris, 1920–1925). Met Pablo Picasso. Developed artistic foundation. European-influenced works
New York, USA 1927–1962 International career flourished. First showed at The New School (1931). Created murals including "Ecuadorian Festival" (1932). Director of Art Workshops (1935), then Director of Art Department until death. Created mural for Ecuadorian Pavilion at 1939 World's Fair. Ecuadorian Festival mural (1932), World's Fair mural (1939)
