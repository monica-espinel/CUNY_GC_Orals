David Alfaro Siqueiros

David Alfaro Siqueiros (1896–1974) was a pivotal figure in modern Mexican art and one of "Los Tres Grandes" alongside Diego Rivera and José Clemente Orozco. He profoundly shaped the Mexican Mural Movement through his revolutionary politics, technical innovations, and dramatic public murals addressing social justice and class struggle.

Los Angeles, USA 1932 Painted controversial murals. Developed new materials and techniques including concrete for frescoes, airbrushing, and camera projection for wall designs. América Tropical (1932), Street Meeting, Portrait of Mexico Today
New York, USA 1936 Founded Siqueiros Experimental Workshop. Taught modern techniques influencing Jackson Pollock and Abstract Expressionism. Experimental works, teaching influence on American artists
