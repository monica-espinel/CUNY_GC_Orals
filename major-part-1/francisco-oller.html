Francisco Oller

Francisco Oller (1833–1917) was a pioneering Puerto Rican painter known for his essential role in Realism and Impressionism, uniquely bridging European avant-garde art with the Caribbean's cultural reality. The only Latin American actively contributing to the development of Impressionism alongside Monet and Pissarro, his masterpiece El Velorio (1893) remains one of the most important works in Puerto Rican art history.
