Hector Hyppolite

Hector Hyppolite (1894–1948) is celebrated as the Grand Maître (Grand Master) of Haitian art and a key figure in Caribbean modernism. A self-taught artist and third-generation Vodou priest (houngan), his work transformed the perception of Haitian painting through vivid, spiritually charged images rooted in Vodou themes.
