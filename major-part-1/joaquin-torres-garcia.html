Joaquín Torres García

Joaquín Torres García (1874–1949) was a groundbreaking Uruguayan-Spanish artist, theorist, and educator whose career spanned continents. He founded Constructive Universalism, a movement melding geometric abstraction, universal symbolism, and indigenous American influences. His iconic inverted map "América invertida" (1943) proposed a new artistic hierarchy led by Latin America.
