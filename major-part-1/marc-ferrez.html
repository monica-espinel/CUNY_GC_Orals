Marc Ferrez

Marc Ferrez (1843–1923) was one of Brazil's most renowned photographers, celebrated for his role in documenting the country's transformation during the late 19th and early 20th centuries. His panoramic views of Rio de Janeiro and technical innovations in landscape photography made him a master whose work provides invaluable historical records of Brazil's modernization.

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil 1843 (birth); main career Born into family of French artists. Apprenticed at Leuzinger Studio. Opened own studio in 1865. Became official photographer of Brazilian Imperial Navy. Documented urban and natural landscapes of Rio extensively. Panoramic views of Rio, Avenida Central, Avenue of Royal Palms
Paris, France 1873; periodic visits Returned to France to acquire new photographic equipment including panoramic camera after fire destroyed his studio. Mastered panoramic technique. Technical innovations
Throughout Brazil 1870s–1900s Traveled extensively on scientific and ethnographic expeditions, including Geological Commission led by Charles Frederick Hartt. Documented railways, bridges, ports, plantations. Railway construction albums, ethnographic portraits of Bororo Indians
