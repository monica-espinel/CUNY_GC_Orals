Pedro Figari

Pedro Figari (1861–1938) was one of Uruguay's most influential artists, as well as a lawyer, writer, and politician. Not devoting himself fully to art until age 60, he is best known for his distinctive early modernist style emphasizing scenes of everyday life, local customs, colonial patios, folk dances, gaucho life, and Afro-Uruguayan cultural traditions—especially candombe celebrations.
