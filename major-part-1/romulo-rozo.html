Rómulo Rozo

Rómulo Rozo (1899–1964) was a pioneering Colombian sculptor whose work merges Art Deco aesthetics with pre-Columbian Colombian (particularly Muisca) mythology and motifs. His iconic sculpture Bachué (1925) sparked the "Bachués" literary and art movement in Colombia, calling for art rooted in local traditions rather than European academic conventions.
