Tarsila do Amaral

Tarsila do Amaral (1886–1973) was one of the most influential artists in Brazilian modernism, whose work played a pivotal role in shaping a distinctly national artistic identity. Her iconic painting Abaporu (1928) directly inspired the Antropofagia (Anthropophagy) movement, which advocated "cultural cannibalism"—digesting and transforming European influences to create a unique Brazilian identity.
