Information

Field Value
Artist Allora & Calzadilla (Jennifer Allora & Guillermo Calzadilla)
Dates Jennifer Allora: 1974–present; Guillermo Calzadilla: 1971–present
Country United States (Allora), Cuba (Calzadilla); based in Puerto Rico
Movement Conceptual Art, Performance Art, Sound Art, Installation Art
Keywords (bibliography) Colonialism, militarism, ecology, power, Caribbean identity, Vieques, inscription/erasure, presence/absence
Key scholars Yates McKee (political ecology and art), Claire Bishop (participatory art), Nato Thompson (socially engaged art)
Influenced by Caribbean colonial history, US military occupation of Vieques, Puerto Rican identity and culture
Studied Under Allora: University of Richmond (BA, 1996), MIT (MS, 2003); Calzadilla: Escuela de Artes Plásticas, Puerto Rico (BFA, 1996), Bard College (MFA, 2001)

Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Florence, Italy 1995 (meeting) The two artists met while studying abroad in Florence, marking the beginning of their collaboration. Early collaborative experiments
San Juan, Puerto Rico 1995–present (primary residence) Their adopted home base. Puerto Rico's complex colonial history and the US military presence on Vieques profoundly shaped their work on land, power, and activism. The Caribbean context informs their exploration of colonialism and cultural identity. Land Mark series (1999–2010), Returning a Sound (2004), Under Discussion (2005), Puerto Rican Light (Cueva Vientos) (2015)
New York, USA Various exhibitions and collaborations Exhibited widely in major New York institutions; the city's art scene influenced their conceptual approach and international visibility. Stop, Repair, Prepare (2008), works at Guggenheim Museum

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
