Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Havana, Cuba 1948–1961 (birth to age 12) Birthplace. The trauma of forced exile and separation from homeland became a persistent theme throughout her work, shaping her exploration of identity, belonging, and cultural displacement. Themes of exile and return permeate entire oeuvre
Iowa, USA 1961–late 1970s (education and early career) Arrived as child refugee through Operation Pedro Pan. Studied painting then joined Intermedia program at University of Iowa under Hans Breder. Here she developed her earth-body art practice, seeking art that felt "real enough" using her body, natural materials, and elemental forces. Silueta Series (1973–1980), Imagen de Yagul (1973), Untitled (Body Tracks) (1974)
Mexico 1971, 1973–1978 (various visits) Traveled to Mexico multiple times, creating works at ancient Mesoamerican archaeological sites. The connection to pre-Columbian culture and Zapotec tombs influenced her ritual-based practice. Imagen de Yagul (1973), Mexican Siluetas
New York, USA Late 1970s–1985 Moved to New York to be part of the larger art scene. Continued her practice and gained international recognition. Married sculptor Carl Andre in 1985. Later works, gallery exhibitions
Rome, Italy 1983 Awarded Rome Prize fellowship at American Academy in Rome. Created sculptural works during this residency. Sculptural experiments, Rupestrian Sculptures
