Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil 1933–present (primary residence, with periods abroad in 1954–55 and 1969–70) Birthplace and lifelong home base. The city's cultural scene and proximity to the Museum of Modern Art (MAM-Rio) influenced her abstract and neo-concrete work. The politically turbulent environment of the 1960s–70s shaped her conceptual and critical approach to Brazilian identity. Raio-X de um olho (1968), Native Brasil/Alien Brasil (1977), A Pão Nosso de Cada Dia (1978), Circe (2006)
New York, USA 1954–1955 (studies); 1969–1970 (teaching) First visit: Studied art history at the Metropolitan Museum of Art and attended classes at the New School for Social Research and NYU. Second visit: Visiting teacher at Columbia University. Exposure to American conceptual art and video art influenced her experimental media approaches. História do Brasil: Little Boys & Girls (1975), early photomontage experiments
Paris, France 1967 (and subsequent visits) Exhibited at the Biennale of Young Artists in 1967, gaining international recognition. Paris exposure influenced her engagement with global art movements and reinforced her exploration of identity and cultural critique in a cosmopolitan context. Prints and engravings from the 1960s, Fronteiriços series (1990s)
