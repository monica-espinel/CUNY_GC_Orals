Artist Beatriz González

Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Bucaramanga, Colombia 1938–late 1950s (birth and childhood) Birthplace in the Santander region. Early exposure to Colombian provincial life and local visual culture shaped her concept of "provincial art" as distinct from European or American Pop Art. Early formative period
Bogotá, Colombia 1958–present (primary residence) Studied at Universidad de los Andes where she met mentors Marta Traba and Juan Antonio Roda. The politically turbulent environment of La Violencia and later conflicts profoundly influenced her engagement with newspaper imagery, political critique, and social memory. Los Suicidas del Sisga (1965), La Última Mesa (1970), Decoración de Interiores (1981), Auras Anónimas (2009)
Amsterdam, Netherlands 1966 (studies) Studied printmaking at the Rijksakademie van beeldende kunsten while her husband received a grant in Rotterdam. Deepened her technical skills and expanded her engagement with international art movements. Printmaking experiments, early furniture pieces

2009 Auras Anónimas Installation (Cemetery Columbarium) Bogotá – Installation at Central Cemetery covering 8,957 columbarium niches with images of anonymous victims Memorial installation addressing violence and anonymous victims in Colombia
