Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Santiago, Chile 1948–1973 (birth to exile) Birthplace. Studied architecture then fine arts at University of Chile. Developed "Arte Precario" concept in mid-1960s. The political climate under Salvador Allende and subsequent coup profoundly shaped her political consciousness and artistic practice. Early Precarios, foundational conceptual works
London, England 1973–late 1970s (exile) Went into exile after the military coup against Allende. Studied at Slade School of Fine Arts. The experience of displacement and exile became central themes in her work. Exile period works, early poetry publications
Bogotá, Colombia 1970s–1980s Lived in Colombia during period of exile. Continued developing her practice and connections to Latin American art communities. Collaborative and performance works
New York, USA 1980s–present (primary residence) Based in New York since the 1980s. Major quipu installations and international recognition developed here. Active in feminist and activist art communities. Quipu Womb (2017), Palabrarmas, major museum exhibitions

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1966–present Precarios Found objects, debris, natural materials Chile and worldwide – Ephemeral assemblages made from debris and natural materials, designed to disappear back into the landscape; reflect impermanence and fragility
1970s–present Palabrarmas (Word-Weapons) Visual poetry, drawing, text Various – Conceptual drawings that blur boundaries between visual art and literature; words transformed into tools of resistance Visual poetry exploring language as political tool
1980s–present Quipu Series Textile Installation (knotted wool) International – Large hanging sculptures of knotted and spun wool inspired by Andean record-keeping; "poems in space" bridging memory, ritual, and resistance
2017 Quipu Womb (The Story of the Red Thread) Site-specific textile installation Athens/Kassel – Created for documenta 14; monumental quipu installation addressing memory, loss, and colonial erasure
2018 Disappeared Quipu Textile Installation Brooklyn Museum, Boston – Large-scale quipu addressing historical erasure and indigenous memory Monumental quipu installation exploring absence and presence
2022 Brain Forest Quipu Textile Installation Tate Modern, London – Site-specific installation in Turbine Hall addressing ecological crisis and indigenous knowledge Large-scale installation merging art and environmental activism
