Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks

Insertions into Ideological Circuits (1970–76), Red Shift (1967–84), Southern Cross (1969–70)
Brasília, Brazil 1960s (studies) Studied art in Brasília. Influenced by Neo-Concrete artists and the modernist aspirations of the new capital city. Early conceptual experiments
New York, USA Various periods Studied and exhibited in New York. Exposure to international conceptual art movements reinforced his experimental approach. International exhibitions and collaborations

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1967–84 Red Shift Installation Brazil – Three rooms saturated in red with objects, spilled liquid, and running red-colored water; prompts reflection on presence, consumption, and transformation
1969–70 Southern Cross Sculpture (wood cube) Brazil – Tiny 9mm wooden cube referencing indigenous Tupi beliefs about fire; placed alone in empty room, addresses cultural disappearance
1970–76 Insertions into Ideological Circuits: Coca-Cola Project Conceptual Art (bottles with silkscreened text) Brazil – Coca-Cola bottles with subversive messages and Molotov cocktail instructions silkscreened, returned to circulation; resistance during military censorship
1970–76 Insertions into Ideological Circuits: Banknote Project Conceptual Art (stamped currency) Brazil – Currency stamped with political messages ("Who killed Herzog?", "Yankees Go Home", "Direct Elections") and returned to circulation Brazilian currency as vehicle for political dissent during dictatorship
1970–75 Eureka/Blindhotland Installation Brazil – 200 black balls of identical volume but varying mass; viewers discover differences between appearance and reality through interaction
1987 Mission/Missions (How to Build Cathedrals) Installation Brazil – Installation with bones, coins, and communion wafers addressing colonialism and indigenous genocide Installation addressing colonial violence and religious conversion
