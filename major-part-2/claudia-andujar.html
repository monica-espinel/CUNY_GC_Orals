Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Neuchâtel, Switzerland 1931 (birth) Birthplace. Born to a Hungarian Jewish father and Swiss Protestant mother. Experienced trauma of WWII – father and much of his family perished in Nazi concentration camps. Early formative period
New York, USA Post-WWII (studies) Emigrated to New York after the war. Studied humanities at Hunter College. Began photojournalism career. Early photojournalistic work
São Paulo, Brazil 1955–present (naturalized 1976) Moved to Brazil to join her mother. Worked as photojournalist for Realidade magazine. Adopted country became focus of life's work. Early Brazilian documentary photography
Yanomami Territory, Brazilian Amazon 1971–present (ongoing documentation) Pivotal encounter with Yanomami people in 1971 began lifelong project. Developed experimental photographic techniques (infrared film, Vaseline-blurred lenses) to capture spiritual and ritual life. Close relationships with Yanomami leaders shaped activist photography practice. Yanomami Series, Marcados (Marked), Sonhos (Dreams)
