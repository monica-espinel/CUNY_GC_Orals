Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Bogotá, Colombia 1958–present (birth and primary residence) Birthplace and lifelong home. Colombia's decades-long history of violence, civil war, and forced disappearances profoundly shaped her practice. Extensive research with survivors and victims' families grounds her work in real testimony. Returned after NYU to teach and focus on social issues. Atrabiliarios (1992–2004), La Casa Viuda (1994–95), Sumando Ausencias (2016), Fragmentos (2018)
New York, USA Graduate studies Earned MA from New York University. Exposure to international contemporary art discourse and theoretical frameworks influenced her conceptual approach. Theoretical development of practice
London, England 2007 (Tate Modern commission) Created iconic Shibboleth crack in Turbine Hall floor, addressing immigration, racism, and exclusion on international stage. Shibboleth (2007)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1992–2004 Atrabiliarios Shoes, animal bladders, surgical thread Colombia – Worn women's shoes sealed in wall niches with animal bladders; represents the disappeared and their silenced lives
1994–95 La Casa Viuda (Widowed House) Found furniture, fabric, bone Colombia – Assemblages of domestic objects (doorframes, furniture, children's items) embedded in one another; evokes trauma and dislocation of victims' families
2007 Shibboleth Installation (crack in floor) Tate Modern, London – 167-meter crack through Turbine Hall floor representing immigration, racism, and exclusion; colonial and contemporary boundaries
2008–10 Plegaria Muda (Silent Prayer) Installation (paired tables with earth and grass) Colombia – Paired tables filled with earth and grass referencing mass graves; dignifies memory of anonymous victims of extrajudicial executions
2016 Sumando Ausencias (Adding Absences) Participatory Installation (cloth panels) Bogotá – Victims' names sewn onto white cloth panels covering Plaza de Bolívar; act of public mourning during peace process Participatory memorial covering Bogotá's main plaza
2018 Fragmentos Installation (floor from melted weapons) Bogotá – Exhibition space floor crafted from melted metal of surrendered FARC weapons; memorial marking end of civil war and peace process Permanent counter-monument made from surrendered weapons
