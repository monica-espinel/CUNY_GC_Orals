Information

Field Value
Artist Félix González-Torres
Dates 1957–1996
Country Cuba (born), Puerto Rico, United States
Movement Minimalism, Conceptual Art, Participatory Art, AIDS activism art
Keywords (bibliography) Public/private body, loss, memory, AIDS, love, viewer participation, impermanence, democracy, intimacy
Key scholars Nancy Spector (Guggenheim), Julie Ault (Group Material), Miwon Kwon (site-specificity), Joshua Chambers-Letson
Influenced by Minimalism, critical theory, Whitney Independent Study Program, AIDS crisis, personal loss (death of partner Ross Laycock)
Studied Under Pratt Institute, New York (BFA Photography, 1983); International Center of Photography/NYU (MFA, 1987); Whitney Independent Study Program

Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Guáimaro, Cuba 1957 (birth) Birthplace. Cuban-American identity remained central to his work and the experience of displacement. Themes of exile and identity permeate work
Madrid, Spain Brief period (age 13) First moved to Madrid after leaving Cuba before settling in Puerto Rico. Transitional period
Puerto Rico 1970–1979 Began art studies in Puerto Rico before moving to New York. Early art education
New York, USA 1979–1996 (primary residence) Arrived on scholarship to Pratt Institute. Whitney Independent Study Program prompted interest in conceptual art and critical theory. Member of Group Material collective (1987–1991). New York art scene and AIDS crisis profoundly shaped his practice of intimate, participatory works addressing loss and memory. "Untitled" (Perfect Lovers) (1987–90), "Untitled" (Portrait of Ross in L.A.) (1991), "Untitled" (Billboard) (1991)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1987–90 "Untitled" (Perfect Lovers) Two synchronized clocks New York – Two identical clocks side by side, eventually falling out of sync; metaphor for relationship, time's passage, and fragility of life
1991 "Untitled" (Portrait of Ross in L.A.) Individually wrapped candies New York – Pile of candy weighing 175 pounds (Ross's ideal body weight); viewers invited to take pieces; symbolizes loss, consumption, remembrance, and AIDS crisis
1991 "Untitled" (Billboard) Billboard (photograph) Manhattan – Photograph of rumpled bed installed on 24 billboards across New York; alludes to domestic intimacy, absence, and loss of partner
Various Paper Stacks Stacks of offset prints on paper Various venues – Stacks of identical prints; viewers invited to take sheets; works on impermanence, participation, and endless supply Participatory installations inviting viewers to take paper
Various Light Strings Light bulbs on electrical cord Various venues – Strings of light bulbs draped in space; evoke celebration, domesticity, and inevitability of burnout
