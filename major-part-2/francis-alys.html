Artist Francis Alÿs

Country Belgium (born), Mexico (based since 1986)
Movement Conceptual Art, Performance Art, Social Practice
Keywords (bibliography) Walking, urban intervention, geopolitics, paseos, poetry and politics, futility, collective action, storytelling, rumor
Key scholars Cuauhtémoc Medina (curator), Russell Ferguson (critic), Mark Godfrey (Tate curator)

Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Antwerp, Belgium 1959 (birth) Birthplace. Studied architecture in Belgium and Italy. European training provided foundation in spatial thinking. Architectural background
Mexico City, Mexico 1986–present (primary residence) Moved to participate in post-earthquake reconstruction, which profoundly shaped his sensitivity to urban form and social dynamics. Transitioned from architecture to art. Mexico City's streets, informal economies, and urban tensions became central subjects of his practice. Paradox of Praxis 1 (1997), The Collector (1990–92), Children's Games series
Lima, Peru 2002 (When Faith Moves Mountains) Created collective action piece with 500 volunteers on outskirts of Lima, addressing Peru's political situation. When Faith Moves Mountains (2002)
Jerusalem / Israel-Palestine 2004 (The Green Line) Reprised paint-dripping walk along 1948 armistice line, addressing borders and divisions. The Green Line (2004)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1990–92 The Collector Performance, Video Mexico City – Walked through city pulling magnetized toy dog on wheels, accumulating metallic debris; commentary on informal economies and urban refuse
1997 Paradox of Praxis 1 (Sometimes Making Something Leads to Nothing) Performance, Video Mexico City – Pushed large block of ice through streets for nine hours until it melted; explores futility, labor, resistance, and artistic production
2002 When Faith Moves Mountains Social Practice, Video Lima, Peru – 500 volunteers used shovels to collectively move a sand dune by a few inches; allegory for collective action and imperceptible impact
2004 The Green Line (Sometimes doing something poetic can become political...) Performance, Video Jerusalem – Walked along 1948 armistice line dripping green paint; addresses borders, division, and conflict Walking performance along contested border in Jerusalem
1999–present Children's Games Video series Various conflict zones worldwide – Celebrates resilience and creativity of children in areas of conflict and adversity Ongoing video series documenting children's play in conflict zones
