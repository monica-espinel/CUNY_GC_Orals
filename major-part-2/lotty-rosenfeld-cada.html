Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Santiago, Chile 1943–2020 (primary residence) Birthplace and lifelong home. Studied printmaking at Universidad de Chile. Emerged as key figure in "escena de avanzada" – avant-garde network fusing activism, poetry, and conceptual art against Pinochet dictatorship. Co-founded CADA in 1979. Used public streets as sites of resistance and transformation. Una Milla de Cruces Sobre el Pavimento (1979), No + campaign, video works

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1979 Una Milla de Cruces Sobre el Pavimento (A Mile of Crosses on the Pavement) Performance/Public Intervention/Video Santiago – Transformed street dividing lines into crosses using white tape; metaphor for defiance against rigid state order; activated public space as site of resistance
1979–1985 No + (No More) – CADA collaboration Public Intervention/Graffiti Chile – Phrase inscribed in public spaces, completed with grievances ("No + torture," "No + hunger"); became symbol of popular resistance; later adopted by feminist collective Mujeres por la Vida Ubiquitous street phrase becoming rallying cry against dictatorship
1998 El empeño latinoamericano (The Latin American Commitment) Video Chile – Video critiquing neoliberal capitalist practices Video documentation addressing economic policies
Various Cross Interventions (ongoing series) Performance/Video/Photography International – Rosenfeld repeated cross-making intervention in different cities and contexts worldwide, documenting each as photo and video Series of interventions in various international locations
