Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
La Vega, Matanzas, Cuba 1959 (birth) Birthplace in sugar-producing region. Family descended from Nigerians enslaved in Cuba's sugar industry, as well as Hispanic and Chinese immigrants. Grandmother was Santería priestess; father was herbalist. Deep connection to Yoruba-rooted spiritual practices. Early formative period; foundation for Afro-Cuban themes
Havana, Cuba Studies and early career Studied at National School of Art and Universidad de las Artes (ISA). Developed artistic practice grounded in Cuban context before emigrating. Early work developed in Cuban art context
Boston, USA Late 1980s–present Moved to US; earned MFA at Massachusetts College of Art. Became tenured professor at Tufts University. Personal experience of exile and diaspora became central themes. Works explore displacement, cultural resilience, and immigrant experience. Los Caminos (The Path) (1997), Sugar/Bittersweet (2010), Spoken Softly with Mama
Nashville, USA Recent years Cornelius Vanderbilt Endowed Chair at Vanderbilt University. Founded Engine for Art, Democracy & Justice Program. Continued practice and academic leadership
