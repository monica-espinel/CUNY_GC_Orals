Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Buenos Aires, Argentina 1943–present (primary residence) Birthplace and home. Studied at Escuela de Bellas Artes. Created immersive happenings and installations responding to Argentine political and cultural context. Works address dictatorship, censorship, and national identity. La Menesunda (1965), El Obelisco de Pan Dulce (1979), Parthenon of Books (1983)
Paris, France 1960s (formative period) Formative years in Paris exposed her to Nouveaux Réalistes and European avant-garde. Contacts led to exploration of demystifying art objects and pushing viewer participation. Created La Destrucción (1963). La Destrucción (1963), Colchones series
New York, USA 1960s (artist residencies) Engagement with New York art scene and Pop Art. Connections with Andy Warhol led to later satirical works. Payment of the Argentine Foreign Debt to Andy Warhol with corn (1985)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1963 La Destrucción (The Destruction) Happening/Performance Paris – Invited artists to destroy her installations; radical approach to artistic consumption and demystification of art object Participatory destruction event challenging art conventions
1965 La Menesunda Immersive Installation Buenos Aires – Immersive labyrinth of sensory environments created with Rubén Santantonín; landmark installation pushing public engagement
1979 El Obelisco de Pan Dulce (The Sweet Bread Obelisk) Participatory Installation Buenos Aires – National monument replica made of sweet bread; eventually eaten by public; commentary on national symbols and mass participation Edible monument inviting public consumption
1983, 2017 The Parthenon of Books Monumental Installation Buenos Aires (1983); Kassel (2017, Documenta 14) – Structure built from thousands of banned books; books later distributed freely; symbol of freedom of expression and political change
1985 Payment of the Argentine Foreign Debt to Andy Warhol with corn Performance/Satire New York – Satirical act "paying" Andy Warhol with Argentine corn; highlighting geopolitical and economic situation Satirical performance addressing Argentina's international position
