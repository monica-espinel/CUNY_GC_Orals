Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Danzig (Gdańsk), Germany/Poland 1915 (birth) Birthplace. Grew up in Berlin. Studied philosophy and art history. Left Germany to escape WWII. Early education and formation
Morocco, Spain 1940s (exile) Lived in Morocco and Spain after fleeing Germany before moving to Mexico. Transitional period
Mexico 1949–1990 Moved to Mexico in 1949. Joined School of Architecture in Guadalajara; founded Iberoamerican University's School of Plastic Arts. Blended German Expressionism with geometric abstraction. Pioneer of Mexican modern art and "Emotional Architecture." Museo Experimental El Eco (1953–54), Torres de Satélite (1957–58), La Serpiente de El Eco (1953)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1953–54 Museo Experimental El Eco Architecture/Installation Mexico City – "Total work of art" integrating architecture, sculpture, and choreographed visitor experience; rough materials, dramatic voids, shifting scales designed to evoke introspection and awe
1953 La Serpiente de El Eco (The Serpent of El Eco) Steel sculpture Mexico City – Large steel sculpture for El Eco Museum Monumental steel sculpture at El Eco
1957–58 Torres de Satélite Concrete towers (public sculpture) Ciudad Satélite, Mexico City – Five colorful concrete towers (30–52 meters) created with Luis Barragán and Chucho Reyes; totemic symbol of Mexico's modernization; gateway to suburban development
1968 La Ruta de la Amistad (Friendship Route) Curated public sculpture project Mexico City – Series of monumental international sculptures along ring road for 1968 Olympic Games; commissioned and curated by Goeritz Monumental sculpture route for Mexico City Olympics
1980 Corona del Pedregal (Crown of Pedregal) Sculpture Mexico City – Monumental work continuing integration of art and urban space Late monumental work
