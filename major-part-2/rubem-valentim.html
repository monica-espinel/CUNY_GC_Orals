Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Salvador, Bahia, Brazil 1922 (birth)–1950s Birthplace in heart of Afro-Brazilian culture. Self-taught; began creating art as child. Deeply influenced by Bahian Candomblé and Umbanda religious traditions. Key member of Movement for the Renewal of Plastic Arts in Bahia. Early paintings incorporating Afro-Brazilian symbols
Rio de Janeiro, Brazil 1950s–60s Moved to Rio and engaged with Brazilian avant-garde. Received fellowship for travel abroad. Development of mature geometric style
Rome, London 1960s (fellowship) Exposure to African art and Third World political movements intensified interest in cultural synthesis and decolonial orientation. Participated in First World Festival of Negro Arts in Dakar, Senegal (1966). International exposure and theoretical development
Brasília, Brazil Later career Moved to new capital; continued monumental and public works. Monumental sculptures and public commissions
