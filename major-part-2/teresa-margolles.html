Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Culiacán, Sinaloa, Mexico 1963 (birth) Birthplace in heart of drug trafficking region. Early experience of violence shaped her lifelong focus on death and social violence. Regional context for violence themes
Mexico City, Mexico 1990s–present Trained in forensic science and communication. Worked in morgues observing effects of drug-related violence, poverty, and governmental neglect. Founded SEMEFO collective (named after forensic medical service). Developed practice using materials from morgues and crime scenes. En el Aire (In the Air) (2003), 127 cuerpos (2006), La Promesa (2012)

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
2003 En el Aire (In the Air) Installation (soap bubbles) International – Space filled with soap bubbles made from water used to clean bodies in morgue; making invisible consequences of violence tangible Ephemeral bubbles carrying traces of the dead
2006 127 cuerpos (127 Bodies) Textile Installation Mexico/International – Threads soaked with body fluids from autopsy procedures woven into fabric Fabric carrying material traces of violence
2009 ¿De qué otra cosa podríamos hablar? (What Else Could We Talk About?) Installation/Performance Venice Biennale – Fabrics and objects from drug-related murder scenes; performances involving families of victims washing marble floors with water mixed with blood
2010 Sin título (Untitled) – Cement benches Sculpture (concrete) International – Cement benches cast with water used in washing murdered bodies at morgue; appear minimalist but carry unsettling implications Minimalist objects with hidden violence
2012 La Promesa (The Promise) Sculpture (rubble) Mexico – Sculptural block made from rubble of demolished house; evokes loss due to urban violence Sculptural memorial to displacement
