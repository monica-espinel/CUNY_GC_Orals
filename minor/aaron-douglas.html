Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Topeka, Kansas 1899–1917 Birthplace where his mother, an amateur artist, encouraged his early artistic development. Early drawings and sketches
Lincoln, Nebraska 1918–1922 Studied at the University of Nebraska, earning his BFA. Developed foundational artistic skills. Student works
Kansas City, Missouri 1922–1925 Taught art at Lincoln High School while continuing to develop his artistic practice. Early teaching-era works
New York City (Harlem) 1925–1939 Became the leading visual artist of the Harlem Renaissance. Studied under Winold Reiss who encouraged him to explore African art. Developed his signature style of silhouettes with concentric circles and geometric forms. Associated with Alain Locke, Langston Hughes, and other Harlem Renaissance leaders. Aspects of Negro Life (1934), illustrations for God's Trombones (1927), The Creation (1927)
Paris, France 1931 (study) Studied at the Académie Scandinave. Exposure to European modernism and African art in Parisian museums deepened his synthesis of African and modern aesthetics. Works influenced by European modernism
Nashville, Tennessee 1939–1979 Founded the Art Department at Fisk University. Became an influential educator training generations of African-American artists. Continued mural work in the South. Fisk University murals, later paintings and murals
