Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Borgonovo, Switzerland 1901–1919 Birthplace in the Bregaglia valley. Born into an artistic family—his father Giovanni Giacometti was a noted post-Impressionist painter. Early exposure to art shaped his creative foundation. Early drawings and paintings
Geneva, Switzerland 1919–1920 Studied at the School of Fine Arts (École des Beaux-Arts). Began formal training in sculpture and painting. Student works
Italy (Venice, Florence, Rome) 1920–1921 Traveled through Italy studying Renaissance and ancient art. Particularly influenced by Egyptian and Etruscan sculpture, which would inform his stylized figures. Sketches and studies influenced by Egyptian art
Paris, France 1922–1966 (with interruptions) Studied under Antoine Bourdelle at the Académie de la Grande Chaumière. Became involved with Surrealist circles (1930–1935), creating dreamlike objects. After WWII, developed his signature elongated figure style reflecting existentialist philosophy and postwar trauma. Maintained a small studio at 46 rue Hippolyte-Maindron. Suspended Ball (1930), The Palace at 4 a.m. (1932), Walking Man I (1960), Standing Woman series
Stampa, Switzerland 1942–1945 (wartime) Returned to Switzerland during WWII. During this period, his figures became increasingly miniature, reflecting his struggle to capture reality and perception. Miniature sculptures from wartime period
