Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
St. Petersburg, Russia 1891–1902 Birthplace. Born to a working-class family; father was a theatrical props maker. Early childhood
Kazan, Russia 1902–1914 Studied at the Kazan Art School. First encountered Malevich's Suprematism, which deeply influenced his turn toward abstraction. Early student works
Moscow, Russia 1914–1956 Studied at Stroganov Institute. Met Varvara Stepanova, his lifelong collaborator and wife. Became central figure in Russian Constructivism after the 1917 Revolution. Led the Institute of Artistic Culture. Created revolutionary graphic design, photography, and film posters. Declared the "end of painting" with monochrome works at the 5x5=25 exhibition (1921). Pure Red Color, Pure Yellow Color, Pure Blue Color (1921), Spatial Constructions (1920–21), Books (Poster) (1924), Portrait of Mayakovsky (1924), Girl with Leica (1934)
