Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania 1928–1949 Birthplace. Born to Carpatho-Rusyn immigrant parents. Studied pictorial design at Carnegie Institute of Technology. Early exposure to commercial art and illustration. Student works, early drawings
New York City 1949–1987 Moved to NYC and became a successful commercial illustrator for magazines and department stores. In early 1960s, transitioned to fine art, adopting silkscreen printing for mass reproduction aesthetic. Founded The Factory studio, which became a hub for avant-garde artists, musicians, and celebrities. Created iconic Pop Art works exploring celebrity, consumer culture, and the blurring of high and low art. Campbell's Soup Cans (1962), Marilyn Diptych (1962), Brillo Boxes (1964), Chelsea Girls (film, 1966), Shot Sage Blue Marilyn (1964)
