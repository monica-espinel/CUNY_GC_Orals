Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Fox Chase, Pennsylvania 1939–1957 Birthplace. Early artistic interests developed in rural Pennsylvania. Early drawings and paintings
Bard College, New York 1957–1959 Studied poetry and philosophy; completed BA. Developed interdisciplinary approach combining literature, visual art, and performance. Student works
University of Illinois 1959–1961 Completed MFA. Continued exploration of painting and movement. Early paintings
New York City 1961–2019 Became a founding member of Judson Dance Theater. Developed "kinetic theater" blending movement, painting, installation, and performance. Created pioneering feminist body art works using her own body as subject and medium. Associated with Fluxus and the avant-garde scene. Eye Body (1963), Meat Joy (1964), Fuses (1964–67), Interior Scroll (1975), Up to and Including Her Limits (1973–76)
Paris/London (exhibitions) 1964 onwards Performed Meat Joy at Festival de la Libre Expression, Paris, and at the ICA, London. International recognition for radical body-based performance work. Meat Joy performances

Key Works Timeline

Year Artwork Medium Location Context Image
1963 Eye Body: 36 Transformative Actions Performance/Photography New York – Pioneer body art integrating artist with assemblage Schneemann covered her nude body with paint, grease, chalk, and plastic, photographed by Erró
1964 Meat Joy Performance Paris/New York/London – Visceral group performance with bodies, meat, and paint Performers interacting with raw fish, chickens, sausages, wet paint, and plastic in ritualistic celebration
1964–67 Fuses Film New York – Experimental film of intimate sexuality, hand-painted and baked 18-minute film challenging representations of female sexuality in art
1975 Interior Scroll Performance East Hampton – Iconic feminist performance Schneemann extracted and read from a scroll hidden in her vagina, asserting female body as source of knowledge
