Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Springfield, Illinois 1943–early 1960s Birthplace. Grew up in the Midwest before moving to California. Early life
Los Angeles, California 1963–1974 Studied at Los Angeles Trade Technical College, Chouinard Art Institute, and Otis Art Institute under Charles White. Created the influential Body Print series, using his greased body pressed against paper to create haunting, politically charged images addressing racial identity and oppression. Body Prints (1968–1979), Injustice Case (1970)
New York City 1974–present Moved to Harlem and became associated with the Black Arts Movement and post-minimalist conceptual art. Creates installations, performances, and sculptures using found materials from urban streets—hair, bottle caps, chicken bones, basketball hoops—subverting stereotypes while critiquing the art market and racism. Bliz-aard Ball Sale (1983), Higher Goals (1986), In the Hood (1993), African American Flag (1990)
