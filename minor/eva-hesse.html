Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Hamburg, Germany 1936–1939 Birthplace to an Orthodox Jewish family. Fled Nazi Germany as a child on the Kindertransport. Early childhood
New York City 1939–1964, 1965–1970 Raised in New York after immigration. Studied at Pratt Institute, Cooper Union, and Yale (under Josef Albers). Became part of the downtown art scene, befriending Sol LeWitt, Donald Judd, Robert Smithson, and others. Developed her mature sculptural practice using latex, fiberglass, and unconventional materials. Hang Up (1966), Accession (1967–68), Expanded Expansion (1969), Untitled (Rope Piece) (1970)
Kettwig an der Ruhr, Germany 1964–1965 Spent 15 months in Germany with husband Tom Doyle. This period was transformative—she began creating relief sculptures and moved from painting toward three-dimensional work. Exposure to industrial materials influenced her later practice. Relief sculptures from Kettwig period
