Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Argentan, Normandy 1881–1900 Birthplace. Grew up in a family of cattle farmers. Initially trained as an architect. Early life
Paris, France 1900–1940, 1945–1955 Studied at École des Beaux-Arts and Académie Julian. Moved to Montparnasse and associated with Cubist painters and the Section d'Or group. The 1907 Cézanne retrospective was transformative. Developed "Tubism"—his personal Cubist style using cylindrical, mechanical forms. WWI service influenced his machine aesthetic. Nudes in the Forest (1909–11), Contrast of Forms (1913), The City (1919), Three Women (Le Grand Déjeuner) (1921)
New York, USA 1940–1945 Fled WWII to New York. Taught at Yale University. Influenced by American urban energy; created works featuring American subjects like cyclists and construction workers. Works from American exile period
Biot, France 1955 (posthumous museum) The Musée Fernand Léger was established in Biot in 1960 to house his works. Legacy collection
