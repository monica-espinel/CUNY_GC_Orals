Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Berlin, Germany 1893–1933 Born and raised in Berlin. Studied at Dresden Academy and Berlin College of Arts and Crafts. Founding member of Berlin Dada with Raoul Hausmann. Central figure in New Objectivity movement alongside Otto Dix and Max Beckmann. Created biting satirical works exposing Weimar society's corruption, militarism, and hypocrisy. The Funeral (Dedicated to Oskar Panizza) (1918), Eclipse of the Sun (1926), Pillars of Society (1926)
New York, USA 1933–1959 Emigrated to escape Nazi persecution. Taught at Art Students League. His American work became more melancholic and pastoral, though he continued political themes. Never fully regained the caustic edge of his Berlin period. American watercolors and paintings
