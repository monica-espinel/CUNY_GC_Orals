Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Volos, Greece 1888–1906 Birthplace to Italian parents. Early exposure to classical antiquity and Mediterranean light influenced his later metaphysical imagery. Early life
Athens, Greece 1900–1906 Studied at Athens Polytechnic under Georgios Roilos and Georgios Jakobides. Developed foundational artistic skills. Student works
Munich, Germany 1906–1909 Studied at the Academy of Fine Arts. Encountered German Symbolist painters Arnold Böcklin and Max Klinger. Read Nietzsche and Schopenhauer, whose philosophies deeply influenced his vision of enigma and melancholy. Early symbolic paintings
Paris, France 1911–1915 Developed Metaphysical Art (Pittura Metafisica). Created his most famous piazza paintings with long shadows, mannequins, and unsettling perspectives. Exhibited at Salon des Indépendants and was noticed by Guillaume Apollinaire. The Enigma of an Autumn Afternoon (1910), The Song of Love (1914), Mystery and Melancholy of a Street (1914)
Ferrara, Italy 1915–1918 Met Carlo Carrà in military hospital. Together they formally founded Metaphysical Painting. Created mannequin and still-life compositions. The Seer (1915), The Disquieting Muses (1918)
Rome, Italy 1918–1978 (with intervals) After 1919, shifted toward neoclassicism and academic realism, distancing from metaphysical style. Later revisited metaphysical themes as "neo-metaphysics." Later classical and neo-metaphysical works
