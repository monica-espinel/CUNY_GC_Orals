Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Le Cateau-Cambrésis, France 1869–1887 Birthplace in northern France. Studied law before turning to art after illness. Early life
Paris, France 1891–1917, various later periods Studied at Académie Julian and École des Beaux-Arts under Gustave Moreau. Encountered Post-Impressionism. Led the Fauvist movement after the scandalous 1905 Salon d'Automne. Developed his bold color palette and simplified forms. Woman with a Hat (1905), The Joy of Life (1906), The Red Studio (1911), Dance (1909–10)
Collioure, France 1905 Spent summer with André Derain; this period was crucial for the development of Fauvism with its bold, non-naturalistic color. Fauvist landscapes and portraits
Morocco 1912–1913 Two visits to Tangier deeply influenced his use of light, pattern, and exotic subjects. Moroccan paintings and drawings
Nice, France 1917–1954 Settled in Nice on the French Riviera. Mediterranean light influenced his palette. Created odalisque paintings and, in later years, his famous paper cut-outs after illness limited his ability to paint. Odalisque series (1920s–30s), Blue Nude II (1952), The Snail (1953), Chapel at Vence designs
