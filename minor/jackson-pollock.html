Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Cody, Wyoming 1912 Birthplace. Grew up in the American West, which influenced his connection to landscape and scale. Early life
Los Angeles, California 1913–1928 Childhood years in California and Arizona. Exposure to Native American art and Southwestern landscapes. Early exposure to art
New York City 1930–1945 Studied at the Art Students League under Thomas Hart Benton. Worked with Mexican muralists, particularly Siqueiros. Underwent Jungian psychoanalysis. Developed symbolic, totemic works influenced by Surrealism and myth. The She-Wolf (1943), Mural (1943)
Springs, Long Island 1945–1956 Moved to a farmhouse with wife Lee Krasner. Developed his revolutionary drip technique, working on unstretched canvases on the floor. Created his most iconic works during this period. This rural setting provided the space for his monumental paintings. Number 1A, 1948, Lavender Mist (1950), Autumn Rhythm (1950), Convergence (1952)
