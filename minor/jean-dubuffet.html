Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Le Havre, France 1901–1918 Birthplace. Grew up in a family of wine merchants. Early distaste for authority and cultural conventions. Early life
Paris, France 1918–1985 (with breaks) Moved to Paris to study at Académie Julian; quickly abandoned formal training. Associated with Juan Gris, André Masson, and Fernand Léger but didn't pursue art professionally until 1942. After WWII, developed Art Brut concept—championing art by children, the mentally ill, and self-taught outsiders. Created textured works with unconventional materials (sand, tar, straw). Later developed L'Hourloupe series (1962–1974) with distinctive black-lined cell patterns. Corps de dame series (1950–51), L'Hourloupe series (1962–74), Monument au fantôme (1969)
