Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Krefeld, Germany 1921–1940 Birthplace. Early interest in natural sciences and art. The mythologized WWII plane crash in Crimea (1944) became central to his artistic identity—claimed to have been saved by Tatars using fat and felt. Early life; formative myth
Düsseldorf, Germany 1947–1986 Studied at Kunstakademie Düsseldorf under Ewald Mataré; later became professor there. Key figure in Fluxus. Developed concept of "social sculpture"—the idea that everyone is an artist and society itself can be shaped creatively. Used fat and felt as signature materials symbolizing warmth, energy, and healing. Dismissed from professorship in 1972 for admitting unregistered students. How to Explain Pictures to a Dead Hare (1965), The Pack (1969), 7000 Oaks (1982)
New York, USA 1974 Performed I Like America and America Likes Me, spending three days in a gallery with a live coyote—exploring myth, nature, and cultural healing. I Like America and America Likes Me (1974)
Kassel, Germany 1982 Launched 7000 Oaks at Documenta 7—planting 7000 trees paired with basalt stones as an ongoing social sculpture project. 7000 Oaks (1982, ongoing)
