Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Rosario, Argentina 1899–1905, 1922–1928, 1940–1947 Birthplace. Father Luigi Fontana was a sculptor. Returned to Argentina multiple times. Published the White Manifesto (Manifesto Blanco) in Buenos Aires (1946), laying groundwork for Spatialism. Early sculptural works; White Manifesto (1946)
Milan, Italy 1905–1922, 1928–1940, 1947–1968 Studied at Technical School of Ornamentation and Sculpture. Trained at Accademia di Brera under Symbolist sculptor Adolfo Wildt. Developed Spatialism in postwar period. Created the Concetto Spaziale (Spatial Concept) series—first puncturing (buchi), then slashing (tagli) monochrome canvases to reveal the space behind, transcending traditional painting. Concetto Spaziale, Attese (1958–68), Concetto Spaziale, La Fine di Dio (1963–64)
Paris, France 1935–1936 Visited Paris and encountered modernist sculpture, meeting Brancusi and Miró. Abstract sculptures influenced by Parisian avant-garde
