Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Philadelphia/New York 1890–1921 Born Emmanuel Radnitzky in Philadelphia to Russian Jewish immigrant parents. Moved to Brooklyn. Adopted name "Man Ray." Studied at Ferrer Center. Met Alfred Stieglitz and Marcel Duchamp. Co-founded New York Dada with Duchamp and Francis Picabia. The Rope Dancer Accompanies Herself with Her Shadows (1916), early readymades
Paris, France 1921–1940 Moved to Paris, joined Surrealists. Became celebrated portrait and fashion photographer for Vogue and Harper's Bazaar. Developed the rayograph (cameraless photography) by placing objects on photosensitive paper. Created solarized photographs. Made experimental films. Photographed major cultural figures. Rayographs (1922 onwards), Le Violon d'Ingres (1924), L'Énigme d'Isidore Ducasse (1920), film Emak Bakia (1926)
Hollywood/New York 1940–1951 Fled Nazi-occupied Paris. Spent WWII years in Los Angeles. Continued painting and photography. Returned to Paris in 1951. American wartime works
Paris, France 1951–1976 Returned to Paris; continued working until death. Late recognition and retrospectives. Late paintings and photographs
