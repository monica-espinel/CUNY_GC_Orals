Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Blainville-Crevon, France 1887–1904 Birthplace in Normandy. Grew up in an artistic family—his brothers were artists Jacques Villon and Raymond Duchamp-Villon. Early life
Paris, France 1904–1915, 1919–1923 Studied briefly at Académie Julian. Associated with Cubist circles. Created scandal with "Nude Descending a Staircase" at 1912 Salon des Indépendants. Developed concept of readymades beginning 1913. Worked on "The Large Glass." Nude Descending a Staircase, No. 2 (1912), first readymades, The Bride Stripped Bare by Her Bachelors, Even (The Large Glass) (1915–23)
New York City 1915–1918, 1942–1968 Arrived in New York as celebrity after Armory Show scandal. Became central figure in New York Dada with Man Ray and Francis Picabia. Submitted "Fountain" to Society of Independent Artists. Later years spent largely on chess while secretly working on "Étant donnés." Fountain (1917), L.H.O.O.Q. (1919), Étant donnés (1946–66)
Buenos Aires 1918–1919 Brief stay; primarily focused on chess. Chess-related activities
