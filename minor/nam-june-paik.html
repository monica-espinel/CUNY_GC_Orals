Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Seoul, Korea 1932–1950 Birthplace. Fled Korean War with family to Japan in 1950. Early life
Tokyo, Japan 1950–1956 Studied music history and aesthetics at University of Tokyo. Wrote thesis on Arnold Schoenberg. Academic period
Germany 1956–1964 Studied music in Munich and Freiburg. Met John Cage (1958), profoundly influencing his turn toward experimental art. Joined Fluxus movement. Created early "action music" and performance works. Began experimenting with television sets. Hommage à John Cage (1959), early TV experiments
New York, USA 1964–2006 Moved to New York; became pioneer of video art. Collaborated with cellist Charlotte Moorman on performance works. Created video installations, sculptures, and large-scale public works exploring global media, television, and communication technology. Coined term "electronic superhighway." Magnet TV (1965), TV Cello (1971), TV Buddha (1974), Global Groove (1973), Good Morning, Mr. Orwell (1984), Electronic Superhighway (1995)
