Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Málaga, Spain 1881–1891 Birthplace. Father was a painter and art teacher who trained young Picasso from childhood. Earliest drawings
Barcelona, Spain 1895–1904 Studied at La Lonja Academy. Associated with Els Quatre Gats café and bohemian circles. Blue Period emerged after friend Casagemas's suicide. La Vie (1903), The Old Guitarist (1903–04)
Paris, France 1904–1973 (with interruptions) Settled in Montmartre. Rose Period featured warmer tones and circus subjects. Co-invented Cubism with Georges Braque (1907–1914). Associated with Gertrude Stein, Henri Matisse, and dealer Ambroise Vollard. Later periods included Surrealism and Neoclassicism. Created Guernica in response to Spanish Civil War bombing. Les Demoiselles d'Avignon (1907), Ma Jolie (1911–12), Guernica (1937), Weeping Woman (1937)
South of France 1946–1973 Settled in Vallauris, Cannes, and Mougins. Explored ceramics, sculpture, and late paintings. Continued prolific output until death. Ceramic works, late paintings and drawings
