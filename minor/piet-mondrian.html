Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Amersfoort, Netherlands 1872–1892 Birthplace. Early artistic training from his father and uncle, both painters. Studied at Rijksakademie in Amsterdam. Early naturalistic landscapes
Amsterdam, Netherlands 1892–1911 Studied and worked as a painter. Early work influenced by Dutch realism, impressionism, and symbolism. Became interested in Theosophy. Evening; Red Tree (1908–10), Gray Tree (1911)
Paris, France 1911–1914, 1919–1938 First stay: encountered Cubism (Picasso, Braque), which transformed his work toward abstraction. Second stay: developed mature Neoplastic style with grids and primary colors. Fled approaching WWII. Composition in Line (1917), Composition with Red, Blue, and Yellow (1930)
Laren, Netherlands 1914–1919 Returned to Netherlands during WWI. Co-founded De Stijl movement with Theo van Doesburg (1917). Developed Neoplasticism theory. De Stijl manifesto and journal contributions
London, UK 1938–1940 Brief stay fleeing Nazi advance. Continued painting. London period paintings
New York, USA 1940–1944 Final period. Inspired by New York's energy and jazz music. Introduced colored lines (replacing black) in late "Boogie Woogie" series. Broadway Boogie Woogie (1942–43), Victory Boogie Woogie (unfinished, 1944)
