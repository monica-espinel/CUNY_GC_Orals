Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Port Arthur, Texas 1925–1943 Birthplace. Served in U.S. Navy during WWII. Early life
Black Mountain, North Carolina 1948–1952 Studied under Josef Albers at Black Mountain College. Met John Cage and Merce Cunningham, beginning lifelong collaborations. The disciplined approach of Albers and experimental openness of Cage shaped his artistic philosophy. White Paintings (1951)
New York City 1953–1970s Developed "Combines" (1954–1964)—hybrid works combining painting with found objects and everyday materials. Created Erased de Kooning Drawing (1953). Used silkscreen to transfer mass media images onto canvas. Broke boundaries between art and life. Became central figure bridging Abstract Expressionism and Pop Art. Bed (1955), Monogram (1955–59), Canyon (1959), Erased de Kooning Drawing (1953)
Captiva Island, Florida 1970s–2008 Established studio on Captiva Island. Continued experimental work and international collaborations through Rauschenberg Overseas Culture Interchange (ROCI) project. ROCI series (1984–1991)
