Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Charlotte, North Carolina 1911–1914 Birthplace. Childhood memories of the South profoundly influenced his later work. Early life; Southern imagery in later collages
Harlem, New York 1914–1988 (with interruptions) Grew up in Harlem during the Harlem Renaissance. Mother was active in community politics. Studied under George Grosz at Art Students League. Became central figure in African American art. Co-founded Spiral group (1963) with other Black artists during Civil Rights Movement. Developed signature collage technique in 1960s, combining photomontage with paint to depict African American life, jazz, and Southern memories. The Prevalence of Ritual series (1964), Pittsburgh Memory (1964), The Block (1971)
Paris, France 1950–1951 Studied philosophy at Sorbonne on GI Bill. Encountered European modernism firsthand. Abstract paintings from Paris period
St. Martin, Caribbean 1970s–1988 Maintained studio on the island. Caribbean light and subject matter influenced late work. Caribbean-themed collages and watercolors
