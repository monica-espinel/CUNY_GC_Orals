Places Lived and Stylistic Influence

Place Period Stylistic Relationship Key Artworks
Moscow, Russia 1866–1896 Birthplace. Studied law and economics before turning to art at age 30. Early exposure to Russian folk art and iconography. Early life; legal career
Munich, Germany 1896–1914 Studied at Anton Ažbe's school and Munich Academy. Founded Phalanx group (1901) and Der Blaue Reiter (1911) with Franz Marc. Seeing Monet's Haystacks and hearing Wagner's Lohengrin inspired his turn to abstraction. Published Concerning the Spiritual in Art (1911). Der Blaue Reiter (1903), Composition IV (1911), Composition VII (1913)
Moscow, Russia 1914–1921 Returned during WWI and Russian Revolution. Involved in cultural administration. Work became more geometric. Revolutionary period works
Weimar/Dessau, Germany (Bauhaus) 1922–1933 Taught at Bauhaus alongside Paul Klee. Published Point and Line to Plane (1926). Developed geometric abstraction with circles, triangles, and precise forms. Composition VIII (1923), Several Circles (1926), Yellow-Red-Blue (1925)
Neuilly-sur-Seine, France 1933–1944 Fled Nazi Germany. Late works incorporated biomorphic forms influenced by Surrealism. Composition X (1939), late biomorphic abstractions
